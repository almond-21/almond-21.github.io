Homeostasis is the ability of living organisms to maintain a stable internal environment in response to external stimuli. It is an essential characteristic of life that enables survival in changing environments. In modern society, however, rapid changes in weather and climate have increasingly confined our living spaces to indoor environments. On days of heavy rain, extreme heat, or severe cold, people avoid outdoor activities, creating a physical distance from nature. At the same time, there has been a growing effort to recreate “nature” within indoor spaces. This reflects humanity’s attempts to artificially recreate an “idealized nature” while remaining disconnected from the real natural world. Now is the time to overcome our disconnection from nature and seek sustainable ways of interacting with the environment. Homeostasis Shelter offers a solution by subtly adjusting unpredictable external stimuli rather than simply imitating nature, providing humans with a comfortable state. It moderates extreme heat and cold, reducing the stimuli experienced by individuals, and is designed to harmonize with the natural environment while maintaining some level of dependence on it. Seoul is a city characterized by four distinct seasons and diverse climatic phenomena. Among its many landmarks, Yongbi Bridge, which spans Jungnangcheon Stream, serves as a pedestrian bridge connecting residential areas with a large park. It is a beloved outdoor space for city residents. In this location, defined by the linear flow of walking, cycling, and the river itself, the goal is to create a stationary shelter—a space where people can pause and rest, breaking away from the continuous motion.
